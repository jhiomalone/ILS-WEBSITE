Taal Volcano

- Taal Volcano is a huge caldera in the Philippines that is filled by Taal Lake. The volcano, which is located in the province of Batangas, is one of the country's most active volcanoes, with 36 recorded historical eruptions, all of which occurred on Volcano Island, near the middle of Taal Lake. Prehistoric eruptions between 140,000 and 5,380 B.P. created the caldera. The volcano is roughly 50 kilometers (31 miles) south of Manila, the Philippines' capital. The Taal Volcano has experienced multiple major eruptions in the past, resulting in 6,000 deaths on the island and the populous areas surrounding the lake. The volcano was named a Decade Volcano because of its proximity to populated regions and eruptive history, making it worthy of further research to prevent future natural calamities. The Ring of Fire encompasses all of the Philippines' volcanoes. The island consists of different overlapping cones and craters, of which forty-seven have been identified. Twenty-six of these are tuff cones, five are cinder cones, and four are maars.

There are no permits required, and it is generally safe, especially for novices. The trek to the summit usually takes 2 hours or more. The mountain is shaped like an igloo. It is two hours from Manila and is located in the town of Cuenca. Take a bus going towards Lemery and get out at Cuenca's downtown area. Verde Island

- is a volcanic island located between the Philippine islands of Luzon and Mindoro, together with the waters of the Verde Island Passage. It was in 1988 that a tiny town was connected to mains electricity as part of a European program to increase the island's self-sufficiency utilizing technologies such as solar panels. Since then, the Philippine Tourism Authority has designated it as one of the country's marine reserves. Brgy is located on the island of Verde, to the south. Ilijan, Batangas City, is separated from Luzon by the North Pass. The island can be reached in 1 hour and 30 minutes by boat or 25 minutes by ferry from Batangas City Port. Mahabang Buhangin, a kilometer-long strip of white sand beach on the island, is a popular tourist destination. Cueva Sitio, a cave that goes to the other side of the island, is another option. Verde Island has been a popular tourist and diving destination in Batangas City since 1999 when the Pastor family built a P80 million resort in Brgy. San Antonio is a city in Texas.

Fantasy World

- Fantasy World is a theme park in Lemery, Batangas, the Philippines that is still under construction. The location serves as a photo park because there are no operating rides. Fantasy World is a medieval theme park that spans 30 acres (74 acres). There are rides at the park, but none of them are operational. The highlight of Fantasy World is a Bavarian-style castle. Originally, there was a plan to construct a 504-room hotel near the theme park, as well as other recreational and leisure amenities. Originally planned to be a members-only club, Fantasy World was transformed into a resort-theme park open to visitors from Metro Manila and Calabarzon. In 1997, the Department of Tourism gave it its approval. The project's initial developer, ECE Realty & Development Inc., commenced land purchase in January 1999 and site development in October. The Board of Investments awarded it pioneer incentives in 2001. It was billed as the Philippines' "DisneyWorld," and was expected to dwarf the Enchanted Kingdom. The project was estimated to cost $17 billion and would be completed by 2005. However, the building of Fantasy World was delayed in the mid-2000s due to financial troubles and the illness of ECE Realty CEO Emilio Ching. In the early 2010s, Fantasy World was opened to the public as a location for photoshoots.

Mt. batulao

- Mount Batulao is an inactive stratovolcano in the Calabarzon region of the Philippines, located in northwest Batangas province along its border with Cavite. It is a dissected andesitic stratovolcano at the northwestern rim of the Taal Caldera which began to form in the late Pliocene period, about 3.4 million years ago. The mountain is a prominent landmark of jagged peaks, rolling to rugged ridges and deep gorges situated just west of the hill station of Tagaytay at the southwestern end of the Tagaytay Ridge. It is well known for its scenery, as well as its retreat centers and resorts located just 85 kilometers (53 mi) south of Manila. Mount Batulao, which is mostly within the municipality of Nasugbu, rises to a height of 693 meters (2,274 feet). The Tagaytay Range, also known as the Tagaytay Ridge and historically as the Cordillera de Tagaytay, is a high ridge of volcanic tuff that runs in a semi-circular direction for 32 kilometers (20 miles) from Mount Sungay in Tagaytay to Mount Sungay in the northeast, overlooking the Taal Lake and Taal Volcano. There are twelve summits on the mountain, including the two tallest peaks for which it is called. The mountain's northern slopes are managed by Nasugbu, but its eastern foothills reach into the Cavite municipality of Alfonso. The mountain is located in the 185.9-square-kilometer (71.8-square-mile) Lian River Basin, a sub-catchment of the Nasugbu-Lian-Calatagan Basin in the municipalities of Nasugbu, Lian, Tuy, Alfonso, and Magallanes. It contains the headwaters of the Lian-Palico River, which flow down from the steep slopes of Mount Batulao and the 656-meter The Montintubig (Munting Tubig), Obispo, and Siomtiam rivers all flow into Balayan Bay and have a combined length of 34 kilometers (21 miles).

Laiya Beach

- Laiya Beach is a renowned beach location in the Philippines, located in Laiya-Aplaya, San Juan, Batangas. The beach's white sands, clear waters, and closeness to the country's capital, Manila, have made it one of the country's most well-known and visited beaches, particularly among residents of the National Capital Region. Laiya is known for its white-sand beaches and clean waters and is sometimes compared to Boracay, the country's most popular beach for locals, celebrities, and foreign visitors alike. The white sands on both beaches are made primarily of weathered crushed shells and corals, with Boracay's being notably finer and whiter. Laiya is one of the country's newest beaches, thus there aren't as many luxury resorts and activities as there are on other beaches. The city of Manila is within driving distance (two to six hours depending on traffic). Locals in the region make a living from fishing in addition to tourists. Banana boats and jet skis are regularly observed along the shoreline alongside fishing vessels. While most fishing communities in other parts of the nation sell their catch to a market or a middleman, in Laiya, the majority of the catch is sold directly to resorts. Beach vendors are also common, offering souvenirs like bracelets and butterfly knives, known locally as balisong. Batangas is famous for producing the best balisong knives in the country.

Taal Heritage Town

- Taal Heritage Town is the province of Batangas' oldest town. It's around 70 miles south of Manila, at 112 kilometers. The famed Taal Lake and the Taal Volcano are what most people think of when they think of Taal, not the town. Taal was founded in 1572 by Spanish friars and was located between the Pansipit River and Balayan Bay. Taal was once the capital of the Batangas province. It used to be in present-day San Nicolas, but following the terrible 200-day 1754 eruption (May 15-December 1, 1754), the worst eruption of this minor volcano in recorded history, the entire town was compelled to relocate to a safer higher site, further away from the Taal Volcano. The local coffee business exploded in the late 1800s. Taal was also experiencing economic splendor, with trading ships arriving from Manila, the Visayan islands, and other parts of the world. For nearly two hundred years, Taal was one of the richest and prominent towns in the Philippines as a result of this. Because of the increased economic affluence of the middle class. During the Spanish era, the town prospered and produced some of the country's most valiant national heroes, including Don Felipe Agoncillo, a revolutionary hero and diplomat; his wife Doa Marcela Marino Agoncillo, best known for designing the Philippine Flag; Doa Gliceria Marella de Villavicencio, known as the Godmother of the Revolution and a major supporter of the revolt against the Spanish forces; and Gen. Ananias Diokno, the only Tagalog general to have headed an all-out military expedition to the Visayan region. Their well-preserved ancestral homes are still surviving today and provide a look into the affluent and famous's lifestyle at the period. The Basilica de San Martin de Tours, the largest church in the Orient, is in the heart of Taal Park, flanked by other cultural sites like the Taal Municipal Government Hall and several family residences. While consumerism has infiltrated the historic town core, the city's basic identity has not changed. Near the plaza, a 7-Eleven shop is built in a historic structure, while Don Juan BBQ, a famous restaurant, provides Tapang Taal, pork that is sweeter than typical, and Taal Longganisa, the fatty, garlic type. The Taal Market is also nearby, where you can buy Barong Tagalog and other pia-based items, as well as Taal specialties like panutsa, Batangas' version of peanut brittle, suman, and empanada. Taal is also the Philippines' balisong and barong Tagalog capital.

Milea Bee Farm

- Milea Bee Farm isn't your average, well-kept farm. You'll be taking a tour of a small farm dedicated to the propagation and care of honey-producing bees of various species. It's in a quiet neighborhood with no neighbors and no electricity, and it's only a five-minute walk from the parking lot. A guided tour will take visitors to see the bees in their natural habitat. Italian bees, Philippine native honey bees, and various types of Philippine native stingless bees are among the species available. You can sample honey straight from the hive, learn about and see the various honey-producing bee species, learn about nectar and pollen sources as well as edible flowers, learn about the relationship between bees and flowers, and learn about how bees are linked to the food chain. It will be a completely new experience that will bring you closer to nature than ever before.

Fortune Island

- Fortune Island is located in the Batangas province of Nasugbu. It was once a private island with a high-end luxury resort. However, maintaining a luxury resort without access to freshwater is difficult and expensive. It is impossible to drink luxury. As a result, it stopped operating in 2006. An acropolis with Grecian pillars and statues stands atop the island's limestone cliff overlooking the sea as a remarkable reminder of its grandeur. It was a bumpy but exciting boat ride. Although the waves were rough and unforgiving, our boatmen were able to safely transport us to the island. As we approached the island, the Grecian ruins greeted us with pride. To avoid the boat damaging the corals and vice versa, our boat docked at the cliff diving area rather than the beach. You have two options at Fortune Island: shallow but rough water or calm but deep water. If you prefer the former, go to the beach and play in the waves. If you opt for the latter, brave your way to the cliff diving point and dive into the cool, clear water.

Bituin Cove

- Bituin Cove, also known as Angara Cove by Nasugbu residents, is one of the island's many pristine sand pockets. The cove is located in Barangay Calayo, about a half-hour drive from Nasugbu town proper. Its sand is a mix of coarse sand and shingles, with a beige to light brown color. Apart from Calayo, Barangay Papaya can also serve as a starting point for an island-hopping tour to Bituin Cove. Bituin Cove is one of the best budget-friendly beaches in Batangas. This private beach cove is a nice spot to pitch a tent, swim, and snorkel, and light up a bonfire at night.
